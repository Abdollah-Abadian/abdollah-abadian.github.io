PhD Thesis — TME-MH Framework

Multi-Class Emotion Classification with Temporal Modeling for Mental Health Inference from Social Media

Supervisor: Prof. Grigori Sidorov · Research Area: Explainable AI (XAI) & Mental Health NLP

This research proposes a Temporal Multi-Class Explainable framework (TME-MH) for detecting and interpreting emotional mental health states from social media text. Rather than the binary "depressed vs. non-depressed" labeling common in prior work, the framework treats fine-grained emotion classification as the primary computational task — grounded in the clinical hierarchy where emotional states are the observable, text-derivable signals from which mental health conditions are inferred.

The approach combines a large-scale longitudinal dataset (100,000+ posts, 5,000+ users, spanning five years across Reddit and X), a deep learning architecture pairing transformer-based text encoding (RoBERTa/DeBERTa) with Bi-LSTM temporal modeling and attention, and systematic integration of XAI techniques (SHAP, LIME, Integrated Gradients) — targeting at least eight emotion classes grounded in ICD-11 and DSM-5 criteria.

Core Contributions

  • A temporally ordered, multi-platform, multi-class annotated dataset filling a resource gap in the field
  • Fine-grained emotion classification (≥8 classes) across Reddit and X, moving beyond binary paradigms
  • A novel temporal architecture explicitly modeling user-level emotional trajectory
  • Cross-platform generalization analysis between Reddit and X
  • A formal XAI evaluation protocol (faithfulness, perturbation, stability) for mental health NLP
  • A clinically motivated framework linking emotion classification to mental health inference
